Warwick Africa Summit is is a student-led organisation which arranges a two-day conference on the social and economic development of Africa. The event hosts 500+ attendees and 20+ speakers annually. These are very high quality speakers including the second lady of Ghana and previously, Aliko Dangote.

Abdullah's main role was as an events executive. As an events executive his main responsibility was to create events, throughout the year, educating people on the current state of Africa to drive ticket sales for the conference. In addition to this, he was also responsible for acquiring African talent for a showcase/gala in between the first and second day of the summit. The aim of this was to display the talent of students at Warwick to the large array of High-profile speakers, driving through the organisation's positive outlook on a future of Africa.

Being primarily a computer science student, an additional role Abdullah took upon himself was to innovate old processes used in the summit. For example, the older marketing strategy included a great emphasis on face to face marketing. Understanding that as students we are less inclined towards this, he worked very closely with the marketing team to create a digital marketing campaign through social media and the organisation's society website.

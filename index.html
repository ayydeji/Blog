WARWICK AFRICA SUMMIT EVENTS EXECUTIVE

During my second year at the University of Warwick I was an Events Executive at Warwick Africa Summit. This is a society to arrange a two-day conference on the social and economic development of Africa, hosting 500+ attendees and 20+ speakers. These speakers included The Second Lady of Ghana and previously Aliko Dangote.


As an events executive my main role was to create events, throughout the year, educating people on the current state of Africa to drive ticket sales for the conference. In addition to this, I was also responsible for acquiring African talent for a showcase/gala in between the first and second day of the summit. The aim of this was to display the talent of students at Warwick to our large array of High-profile speakers, driving through our positive outlook on the future of Africa.


Being primarily a computer science student, an additional role I took upon myself was to innovate old processes used in the summit. For example, the older marketing strategy included a great emphasis on face to face marketing. Understanding that as students we are less inclined towards this, I worked very closely with the marketing team to create a digital marketing campaign through social media and our society website.


The outcome of this is that we were able to sell out of tickets two weeks before the summit took place and following the summit we received a high satisfaction rating from our attendees and speakers.
